凤凰   (Fènghuáng)     into English is "Phoenix Phoenix"

In this compound,
凤 (Fèng) translates to "Phoenix," representing the male aspect of the mythical bird,
凰 (Huáng) also translates to "Phoenix," representing the female aspect.

Together, they symbolize the Chinese phoenix, a creature embodying both yin and yang, and representing virtues like grace, harmony, and noble benevolence in Chinese culture. This duality underscores the bird's significance as a symbol of balance, prosperity, and peace.
